## STEP 5: WRITING GUIDELINES

  

### LANGUAGE AND STYLE:

- Write in clear, simple language suitable for Class 9 students

- Avoid overly complex vocabulary or academic jargon

- Use active voice and present tense for summaries and explanations

- Keep explanations concise but comprehensive

- Write summaries and themes in original words, not copy-pasted from guides

- Ensure continuity and flow between sections

  

### LENGTH GUIDELINES:

- Summary: 6-8 lines (100-120 words)

- Plot points: 1-2 lines each (total 5 points)

- Character descriptions: 3-4 bullet points per major character

- Theme explanation: 4-6 lines (80-100 words)

- Stanza explanation: 2-4 lines per stanza

- Literary device explanations: 1-2 lines per device

- Important line meanings: 2-3 lines per quote

  

### MARKDOWN FORMATTING RULES:

- Use `#` for main chapter/poem title

- Use `##` for major section headers (Summary, Plot, Characters, Theme, etc.)

- Use `###` for sub-sections only if absolutely necessary

- Use `**bold**` for character names, book titles, and key terms (maximum 3 consecutive words)

- Use `-` for bullet points (no nested lists)

- Use `---` as horizontal dividers between major sections

- Use `*italics*` for emphasis sparingly

- Format quotes with `" "` quotation marks

  

### DO NOT INCLUDE:

- Word meanings or vocabulary lists

- Practice questions and answers

- Exam tips or study strategies

- External URLs or references in the notes

- Images, diagrams, or visual elements

- Personal opinions or subjective commentary

- Information not relevant to NCERT textbook content
